Stephanie Bishop Hall

Stephanie Bishop Hall is an associate at Adair Goldblatt Bieber LLP.

Stephanie maintains a broad civil litigation practice.

Prior to joining Adair Goldblatt Bieber LLP, Stephanie completed her articles at the Ministry of the Attorney General – Constitutional Law Branch. She spent her summers at the United Nations in New York and at the Office of the Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court in The Hague.

Stephanie received her law degree from Queen’s University. Throughout her studies she held leadership positions throughout the faculty, including acting as a Program Coordinator for Pro-Bono Students Canada, developing the Queen’s Law Refugee Support Programme, and acting on Faculty Board Committees. She received a number of awards recognizing her outstanding contributions to the faculty.

While in law school, Stephanie developed a passion for advocacy and honed her skills by participating in competitive moots. She was a finalist in the Canadian round of the Phillip C. Jessup International Law Moot Court Competition as well as the first ever Queen’s Law Grand Moot.

Stephanie is an active volunteer with the Refugee Sponsorship Support Program and a member of the Young Advocates Standing Committee Volunteer Roster. She speaks French and Spanish.
